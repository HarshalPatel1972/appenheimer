# Decision Log

> Every significant decision should be recorded once, with its reasoning.

---

# Purpose

This document records major product and engineering decisions made throughout the lifetime of Appenheimer.

Its purpose is to preserve context, prevent repeated debates, and document why decisions were made.

---

# Principles

- Record decisions, not discussions.
- Record the reasoning behind every decision.
- Decisions may evolve, but history should not be rewritten.
- Every significant architectural change should be documented.

---

# Decision Format

Every decision should follow this template.

---

## Decision ID

Unique identifier.

Example:

ADR-001

---

## Title

A short, descriptive title.

---

## Date

When the decision was made.

---

## Status

Choose one:

- Proposed
- Accepted
- Superseded
- Rejected

---

## Context

What problem or question required a decision?

---

## Decision

What was decided?

---

## Rationale

Why was this decision chosen over the alternatives?

---

## Consequences

Positive and negative outcomes expected from this decision.

---

## Related Documents

Reference any relevant documents.

Examples:

- Vision
- Architecture
- Engineering Principles
- Tech Stack
- RFCs

---

# Decision Categories

Examples include:

- Product decisions
- Engineering decisions
- Architecture decisions
- Technology choices
- Search philosophy
- Database changes
- UI principles
- Development workflow

---

# Superseded Decisions

When a decision changes:

- Never delete the old decision.
- Mark it as Superseded.
- Reference the new decision.

Project history should remain intact.

---

# Final Principle

> Future contributors should always understand not only what was decided, but why it was decided.
